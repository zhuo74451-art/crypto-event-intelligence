## Approved slices

### SLICE-001 — Cross-lane compatibility and reuse audit

**State change:** from scattered lane claims and stale integration assumptions to one evidence-linked disposition matrix.

**Observable result:** every relevant A-D and old Lane E module has a `RETAIN`, `ADAPT`, `QUARANTINE`, or `DELETE` disposition; every generic capability has `ADOPT`, `ADAPT`, `BUILD`, or `DEFER`; conflicts and exact refs are recorded.

**Validation:** the matrix covers all declared producer artifacts, integration modules, research modules, scripts, schemas, tests, and reports. No item is accepted only because an old report or test exists.

**Completion gate:** GATE-COMPAT accepts a bounded file allowlist and next route.

### SLICE-002 — Approved Lane E surface and producer contracts

**State change:** from an isolated stale branch with empty producers to a current-branch candidate surface with exact A-D producer locks.

**Observable result:** only approved Lane E files are materialized; producer lock records exact refs, hashes, counts, supported claim boundaries, and no fallback ambiguity.

**Validation:** byte hashes and counts match the declared refs; Lane B state/manifest conflicts are either repaired or explicitly quarantined.

**Completion gate:** all producer-lock checks pass and no stale branch claim remains active.

### SLICE-003 — Real A-D research integration

**State change:** from sample-driven research outputs to one-shot outputs derived from real locked A-D artifacts.

**Observable result:** Research Claims, Evidence Graph nodes and edges, Conflict Sets, Research Questions, and Dossiers preserve source lineage and supported claim boundaries.

**Validation:** no hardcoded sample path, no absolute Windows path, no missing producer, no unsupported statistical or causal assertion, and no recurring service.

**Completion gate:** one-shot integration completes deterministically and emits a complete evidence manifest.

### SLICE-004 — Independent audit and conditional repair

**State change:** from candidate integration to independently falsified evidence.

**Observable result:** audit covers lineage, hashes, counts, schema, path portability, determinism, stale-state rejection, unsupported claims, and clean rebuild.

**Validation:** GATE-INTEGRATION-AUDIT either closes with no modification or compiles one bounded repair package. No Owner input is used for ordinary fixes.

**Completion gate:** all blocking findings are resolved or a terminal blocked state is emitted.

### SLICE-005 — Clean validation and final seal

**State change:** from locally accepted implementation to exact-head reproducible and sealed integration.

**Observable result:** clean-checkout validation, final manifest, execution state, known limitations, and post-seal verification agree on one implementation ref.

**Validation:** deterministic commands pass on the exact ref, tracked files are clean, declared hashes match, and unsupported claims remain false.

**Completion gate:** GATE-FINAL-ACCEPTANCE emits `PROJECT_COMPLETED` or another declared terminal state.

## Minimum, intended, and optional depth

### Minimum success

- WP-001 completes with a full matrix.
- Exact A-D refs and claim boundaries are locked.
- Any unsafe old Lane E surface is quarantined rather than guessed.
- The run stops safely if real integration cannot proceed.

### Intended success

- Real A-D producer artifacts enter the research pipeline.
- Research outputs and evidence lineage are deterministic.
- Clean validation and final seal pass.

### Optional depth inside the same approved boundary

Only when the intended result completes early and evidence remains strong:

- additional fault injection around stale refs and hash drift;
- second clean rebuild;
- path-portability validation;
- no-change closure proof;
- report-versus-Git consistency audit.

No new product feature, service, data source, production route, or infrastructure platform may enter optional depth.

## Reopen conditions

Reopen only the affected slice when:

- a producer ref or artifact hash is wrong;
- a package boundary is proven invalid;
- a deterministic validation cannot be isolated;
- a new fact overturns a locked assumption;
- an external action or expanded approval is required;
- the runtime cannot preserve current-node-only execution.

Do not reopen completed A-D pilot semantics merely because an integration implementation is difficult.
