## Validation evidence

- Every claim references locked producer evidence.
- Every graph edge resolves to a present node.
- Conflict and limitation records are preserved.
- Generated reports distinguish fact, interpretation, hypothesis, and unsupported inference.
- Two consecutive one-shot runs have equivalent logical outputs.

## Cheapest falsification

- Strongest counterexample: The output looks complete but one claim has no valid A-D lineage.
- Cheapest test: Reject any claim whose evidence refs cannot be resolved through producer locks.

## Stop conditions

- Any output requires invented evidence.
- A missing producer is converted to a placeholder success.
- Logical output is nondeterministic without an explained source.
- Unsupported claims cannot be removed without changing product semantics.
