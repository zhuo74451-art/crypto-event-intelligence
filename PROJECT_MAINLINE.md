# Crypto Market Cognition & Signal OS — Mainline Contract

This file defines the canonical product direction for `main`. Runtime and planning state are resolved through `project/CANONICAL_STATE.yaml` and the project files it references.

Earlier roadmaps, handoff packets, RC1 plans, phase plans, one-shot packet framing, and product-positioning documents are historical unless explicitly reactivated by the canonical state.

## Product responsibility

Build an unattended internal crypto-market cognition operator that autonomously:

1. discovers relevant events, themes, mechanisms, and affected assets;
2. verifies point-in-time evidence and source permission;
3. decides whether an item deserves research and machine attention;
4. creates, updates, links, weakens, invalidates, archives, or reopens market theses;
5. maintains asset and theme exposure with time horizon and priced-in state;
6. preserves counterevidence, disagreement, uncertainty, and explicit abstention;
7. allocates bounded retrieval, model, review, and attention resources;
8. reports only material thesis changes or true governance exceptions;
9. learns from longitudinal shadow evidence before judgment components are trusted.

The first user outcomes are autonomous research prioritization and autonomous watch-portfolio maintenance.

The product does not provide copy trading, buy or sell instructions, entries, exits, leverage, sizing, return promises, wallet behavior, order execution, or public recommendations.

## Owner responsibility

The owner is outside the daily judgment loop. The owner retains governance authority for:

- project and coverage boundaries;
- credentials and permissions;
- cost ceilings;
- production and publishing approval;
- wallet or trading authority;
- stop, recovery, and major scope changes.

Ordinary uncertainty is handled by the system through evidence work, abstention, delayed review, downgrade, invalidation, or archive.

## Canonical cognition path

```text
Approved sources and market data
  -> point-in-time evidence and permission checks
  -> event identity and revision history
  -> interpretive claims and causal mechanisms
  -> persistent market theses
  -> asset and theme exposure
  -> priced-in, risk, and disagreement assessment
  -> attention state and machine resource allocation
  -> follow-up evidence and lifecycle transitions
  -> material-change summary or silence
  -> longitudinal shadow evaluation
```

## Core business object

The core object is an **autonomous thesis portfolio**, not a stream of isolated reports.

Every active thesis must preserve:

- evidence and event state;
- mechanism and interpretation;
- affected assets and exposure type;
- time horizon;
- expectations and surprise;
- confirmation and priced-in state;
- supporting and opposing evidence;
- risk and uncertainty;
- attention priority;
- next evidence or catalyst;
- expiry and invalidation;
- revision history.

## Autonomous discovery

The system may create new themes, theses, and asset observations without a prior owner list when all conditions hold:

- the subject remains inside crypto and directly related macro, policy, technology, security, liquidity, and infrastructure mechanisms;
- evidence uses approved public or contracted sources;
- cost and attention budgets remain bounded;
- the discovery has an explicit mechanism, future evidence path, and exit condition;
- no new credential, paid interface, publishing, wallet, or trading authority is introduced.

Out-of-bound findings remain candidates and do not expand live scope.

## Responsibility split

Prefer deterministic code for:

- identity and timestamps;
- hashes and provenance;
- source permission;
- duplicate and conflict checks;
- future-data leakage;
- resource ceilings;
- retry and stop constraints.

Use a small number of bounded semantic roles for:

- thesis formation and revision;
- causal mechanism and exposure;
- time horizon and priced-in interpretation;
- counterevidence and risk attack;
- disagreement-preserving arbitration;
- lifecycle and attention-state decisions.

Every role requires structured inputs, structured outputs, authority limits, and deterministic fallback behavior.

## Stable rules

1. New work follows this contract and the canonical state map.
2. Accepted changes to project identity, responsibility, autonomy, boundaries, or active planning are synchronized to `main` in the same discussion cycle when repository writes are available.
3. Prefer mature packages, isolated services, and thin adapters for generic acquisition, storage, scheduling, notification, and retrieval.
4. Preserve published, effective, updated, first-seen, retrieved, assessment, revision, and review times.
5. Separate evidence, events, interpretations, theses, candidate components, and validated components.
6. QuickFlash remains a separate broad-recall acquisition provider; this repository consumes contracted outputs and may collect direct evidence independently.
7. Require historical out-of-sample and longitudinal real-time shadow evidence before autonomous judgment components are trusted.
8. Preserve explicit insufficient-evidence and abstention behavior.
9. Do not ask the owner to resolve ordinary ambiguity, importance, direction, follow-up, or lifecycle questions.
10. Do not enable paid interfaces, unbounded loops, production publishing, wallet actions, copy trading, or execution without explicit approval.
11. Future work bases must contain the current canonical fingerprint.
12. Review and upgrade existing code only after the autonomous judgment foundation is locked.

## Current business node

- thesis and attention lifecycle;
- autonomous discovery constraints;
- evidence, risk, uncertainty, and abstention rules;
- resource budgets;
- governance exceptions and stop behavior.

Only after this foundation is accepted will the current repository be audited and upgraded.
